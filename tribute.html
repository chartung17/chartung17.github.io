St. Isidore of Seville

Patron Saint of Computer Programmers and the Internet

St. Isidore of Seville, depicted by Bartolom√© Esteban Murillo

Here's a timeline of St. Isidore's life and legacy:

  • c. 560 - Born in Cartagena, Spain
  • c. 600 - Becomes Bishop of Seville
  • c. 600-625 - Writes Etymologiae, the first Christian encyclopedia, consisting of 448 chapters in 20 volumes.
  • 633 - Presides over Fourth National Council of Toledo. Promulgates degree commanding bishops to establish seminaries in their cathedral cities.
  • 636 - Dies after serving more than 32 years as Archbishop of Seville
  • 1470 - Etymologiae becomes the most used textbook in the Middle Ages and is printed in at least ten editions between 1470 and 1530
  • 1598 - Becomes a canonized Saint at the declaration of Pope Clement VIII
  • 1722 - Given the title "Doctor of the Church" by Pope Innocent XIII, a title granted only to the most important teachers in Christian history
  • c. 2000 - Recognized unofficially as the patron saint of computers, computer programmers, and the internet, due to his attempt to record everything ever known in his Etymologiae

"The extraordinary doctor, the latest ornament of the Catholic Church, the most learned man of the latter ages, always to be named with reverence, Isidore"

- Eighth Council of Toledo
